Acquisition Node Inputs

The interlock inputs are read from the 40-pin header J1, the 16-pin header J20, and the 12-pin header J16 of an XM105 card mounted on the HPC FMC connector of an acquisition node.  Signals are LVCMOS (2.5 V) levels.  An input at 0 V corresponds to a logic 0.  Ground pins are available at J16-3 and J16-4.  Note that if J6 jumpers are in place there will be power supply voltages present at J16-1 and J16-2 so be careful connecting test equipment grounds to J16-3 or J16-4.

SFP Assignments

The mitigation node FMC-4SFP+ card must be mounted on the HPC connector of the ZC706.  The rightmost SFP slot when looking into the SFP cages corresponds to link 0.  The next SFP to the left corresponds to link 1, and so on.  The SFP on the ZC706 is used to connect to the MRF compatible event system.  The demonstration system is configured to operate with an event system operating at 2.5 Gb/s.  The mitigation node firmware is capable of receiving data from all eight high-speed serial ports on the HPC connector even though only the FMC-4SFP+ card provides only the first four.  Replacing the FMC-4SP+ card with an eight channel card such as the Faster Technology FMS18 would allow expansion to the full complement of acquisition nodes.

The acquisition node SFP FMC card, if present, must be mounted on the LPC connector of the ZC706.  The SFP used to connect to the next-outermost acquisition node in the chain must be installed in the SFP cage second from the left when looking into the SFP cages.  The SFP on the ZC706 is used to connect to the mitigation node or next-innermost acquisition node in the chain.

The four switches on the mitigation node FMC-4SFP+ card must all be set to '0'.  This configures the FMC-4SFP+ card to provide a 125 MHz reference clock to the high-speed transceivers on the ZC706.
